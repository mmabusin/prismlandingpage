# The Prism — Waitlist Engagement Pack

The full launch-runway comms: the welcome email, a World-Cup-timed nurture drip, the
early-access invite, and the copy for the on-page share buttons. Everything is written to
paste straight into **Loops** (or any ESP). Setup/operating steps live in
[README.md](README.md#waitlist-engagement-loops).

**Voice:** confident, sharp, football-literate, never hypey. The AI is **"The Prism"** —
never "Claude", never "GPT". Wordmark is `The Prism.` with a blue trailing period.
Signature: `— The Prism`.

**Timing context:** the World Cup 2026 hub is **live now** and is the strongest hook we have
during the runway — several touches ride it deliberately. Adjust dates to your actual send calendar.

**Merge fields:** Loops uses `{{firstName}}` / `{{email}}`. We only collect email, so keep a
graceful fallback — e.g. `Hi there` rather than `Hi {{firstName}}` unless you enrich later.

## E3 — Three ways to find any player (Day ~6)

**Subject:** Three ways to find any player
**Preview text:** A ready-made profile, plain-English search, or your own custom filter.

---

Scouting a real target or just settling an argument — either way, digging up the right player
usually means wrestling with spreadsheets. The Prism gives you three ways in. Pick whichever
suits how your brain works.

**1. Start from a ready-made profile.** Choose a position — GK, CB, full-back, DM, CM, CAM,
winger or striker — and start from a proven template. A ball-playing centre-back, a poacher, a
deep-lying playmaker: each one pre-weighted on the stats that actually matter for the role.

**2. Or just describe it — AI Search.** Skip the setup entirely and say what you're after in
plain English — *"a press-resistant defensive midfielder under 24 in Serie A"* — and The Prism
does the filtering for you.

**3. Or build your own.** Open the stat library and click to add up to eight priorities — from
**Finishing**, **xG rate** and **Aerial threat** to **Key ball delivery**, **Through balls** and
**Carrying ability**. Set your hard filters — age range, minimum minutes this season, which of
the top five leagues to include — choose whether to exclude players already on your shortlist,
and run it.

Whichever route you take, you get the same thing back: a ranked, data-backed list — not a wall
of numbers to decode. Save the ones that catch your eye, then line them up and compare side by side.

Next time: what one of those player reports actually looks like up close.

— The Prism

## E4 — Anatomy of a scout report (Day ~10)

**Subject:** What a 5-second scout report actually looks like
**Preview text:** Ask in plain English. Get a scouting department's answer.

---

Say you're after a centre-back. You type:

> *"Find me a CB under 23 in the Bundesliga with elite aerial ability and progressive passing."*

The Prism scans the league, filters by age, ranks by the stats that actually matter for the
role, and hands back a structured report — not a chat blob. Something like:

> **Castello Lukeba** · CB · 21 · 🇫🇷
> **3.4** aerials won /90 · **74%** long-ball accuracy · **4.1** clearances /90 · **7.2** avg rating
> *Reads the game early, steps into midfield without losing his line. Profile fits a
> possession side that defends high.*

Every number is fetched live and normalised per-90, so a player with 900 minutes and a player
with 2,700 are actually comparable. Then you can line two prospects up **side by side**, or drop
one into your **shortlist** and move on to the next.

That's the core loop: a question in, a scout-quality answer out. No stat tables to decode, no
punditry to second-guess.

Next time: a look at what we've been building while you wait.

— The Prism

## Notes for whoever runs this

- **Cadence is a guide, not a rule.** Days 3/8/14/21 are a warm-but-not-annoying rhythm. Tighten
  it if launch is close; stretch it if the runway is long.
- **Ride live moments.** The World Cup is the strongest hook right now — if a specific fixture or
  result is a talking point, a one-off "here's how The Prism called it" send will outperform any
  evergreen email.
- **Keep the promise visible.** Every email should reinforce the one differentiator: *never a
  number from memory — always grounded in live data.* That's the trust that converts a waitlist
  into users.
- **One clear job per email.** Don't stack asks. Each touch does one thing.
